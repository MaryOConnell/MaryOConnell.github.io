Design Inspiration For Your Website

Sample Project : Irish Tourism Website

Deciding on an approach for a design concept is often difficult. A combination of elements, such as colours, shapes and typography can make for an attractive composition. Design aspects such as balance, colour and visual hierarchy should adhere to Gestalt laws. Applying these rules effectively ensures that the information contained in your design is properly communicated.

The following is a hypothetical sketch design for an Irish tourism website. Gestalt principles found in architecture have been applied to the container shell. While certain tasks have to be completed at your desk, it's a good idea to get away from your screen. Looking to fresh sources for ideas can give a unique perspective that you can work in to your design concept.

Sourcing Material

Photographs of historic buildings were sourced and used as a starting point for a sketch design. Modern architectural facades could work equally well. Keeping the rules of Gestalt Theory in mind, head out with a camera or a sketchbook and look for structures that you find appealing. Some guidelines are presented below:
blank line
Architectural and manmade structural forms are often based on a grid system which translates well into web design.
blank line
Elements, such as openings, are often grouped together and set out along this grid. Interpreting this idea for your web design will allow you to lay out your content in a logical and organised manner.
blank line
Architectural features, such as arches and feature windows, are stand alone items that are given prominence and plenty of breathing space. You can draw attention to an important item on your webpage by applying the same rationale.

Sketching Ideas

After gathering images of a selection of buildings that might fit your theme, review them and identify elements that could translate well into your design. This can be a matter of lifting a sketch directly from the facade of a building. Alternatively, you might single out common features and use them as a guide.

In this case, inspiration was drawn from the blocky shapes of the old castles. The wall planes are divided into projections and recesses, giving emphasis to some elements and forcing others into the background. This influenced the design for the slide out menu and the nav bar at the left.
blank line
Images were set out along a grid, reflecting the distribution of castle wall openings, and allowing for adequate white space. The logo is featured in a prominent position on the page, a distance away from the rest of the information.

Colours and typography can be incorporated to further complement the theme. The result adheres to Gestalt laws but retains an architectural identity.
